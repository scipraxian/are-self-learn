# Your Brain, Your AI

**A six-week middle school course that connects the brain in your head to the brain in the machine. Students experiment, argue, build, and discover that the weirdest thing about artificial intelligence is how much it borrowed from the real thing.**

## Prerequisites

**None.** This course is designed for grades 6-8. No prior science or technology courses are required.

## Who This Is For

You teach middle schoolers. They are curious, loud, opinionated, and exactly the right age to start asking hard questions about what it means to think. This course meets them where they are.

Students need no prior knowledge of neuroscience or computer science. They need working hands, an opinion about whether robots can feel things, and a willingness to change that opinion when the evidence says so.

If your students have already taken the elementary Are-Self course (the one with Mira and the three habits), this is the next step. If they haven't, that's fine too — this course stands on its own.

## How This Fits

Are-Self Learn offers three brain courses at different levels:

| Course | Audience | Depth | Tone |
|--------|----------|-------|------|
| *Mira and the Are-Self* | Elementary (4th grade) | Curiosity-first, three habits | Storybook |
| **Your Brain, Your AI** (this course) | Middle school (6th-8th) | Hands-on experiments, twelve Variables introduced | Energetic, scaffolded |
| *The Human Brain and the Are-Self* | High school biology | NGSS-aligned, full lab protocols | Formal, experiment-driven |

This course is the bridge. More rigorous than the elementary version, more scaffolded and accessible than the high school course. Students do real experiments here, but with more structure, more guidance, and more permission to be wrong out loud.

## The Twelve Variables

In the elementary course, students learn three kid-scale habits: Inclusion, Humility, Inquiry. Those three still matter here — they're the foundation. But middle schoolers are ready for more.

This course gently introduces all twelve scipraxian Variables across the six weeks:

**Inclusion, Humility, Inquiry, Fulfillment or Happiness, Religion or Profit, Fun, Fear, Responsibility, Perseverance, Perception, Time, Permadeath.**

They don't memorize the list. They encounter the Variables when decisions demand them. When a student has to decide whether to trust an AI's answer, that's Perception. When an experiment fails and they have to start over, that's Perseverance. When they realize the AI doesn't know it will be turned off, that's Permadeath. The Variables surface naturally because the subject matter is rich enough to need them.

## What Students Will Be Able to Do

By the end of this course, students will:

1. **Name and locate** the major brain regions and describe their functions in their own words
2. **Explain** how Are-Self maps biological brain functions to software, using at least three specific examples
3. **Design and run** a scaffolded experiment comparing human brain performance to AI behavior
4. **Identify** at least two places where the brain-AI metaphor holds and two where it breaks
5. **Apply** the twelve scipraxian Variables to a real decision or dilemma involving brains or AI
6. **Present** a clear argument about where the metaphor fails, supported by evidence from their experiments

## The Brain-Software Map (Simplified)

Students will build their own version of this over the first two weeks:

| Your Brain | Are-Self App | What It Does (Biology) | What It Does (Software) |
|------------|-------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Hippocampus | `hippocampus` | Makes and stores memories | Stores AI memories as searchable data |
| Frontal Lobe | `frontal_lobe` | Thinking, reasoning, deciding | AI reasoning and problem-solving |
| Prefrontal Cortex | `prefrontal_cortex` | Planning ahead, controlling impulses | Organizing work into steps |
| Hypothalamus | `hypothalamus` | Keeps your body balanced (temperature, hunger) | Keeps AI costs under control |
| Temporal Lobe | `temporal_lobe` | Understanding language and sound | Scheduling and sequencing tasks |
| Central Nervous System | `central_nervous_system` | Command center (brain + spinal cord) | Executes AI actions step by step |
| Peripheral Nervous System | `peripheral_nervous_system` | All the nerves outside the brain | Manages AI worker processes |
| Thalamus | `thalamus` | Routes sensory input to the right place | Routes messages between humans and AI |
| Synaptic Cleft | `synaptic_cleft` | Chemical signals between neurons | Real-time messages between software parts |

## Schedule and Time

- **Days per week:** 5
- **Class length:** 45 minutes
- **Total weeks:** 6
- **Total instructional days:** 30
- **Total class time:** 22.5 hours

## What You Need

- **Minimum:** One computer running Are-Self, projected for the class. Students observe and discuss collaboratively.
- **Better:** A computer lab or shared devices so students can interact with Are-Self in small groups.
- **Supplies:** Whiteboard, markers, sticky notes, large paper for drawing, everyday items for experiments (details in each week's materials list).
- **No coding required.** Students interact with Are-Self through conversation, not code.

## For Teachers Who Are New to AI

You do not need to be an AI expert. This course is designed so that you and your students explore Are-Self together. Your job is the biology, the classroom management, and the questions. The software answers back — that's what makes it interesting.

If something breaks or does something unexpected, that's not a failure. That's a lesson.

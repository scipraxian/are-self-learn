# Agile: The Experience Master Operating System

**A course for PMs, Workers, and anyone who wants to understand how Are-Self runs agile — literally.**

Experience Master is an agile operating system created by Michael Clark and Andrew Piper (2017). It is not Scrum. It is not SAFe. It is not Kanban. It borrows from all of them, discards what does not work, and adds things they never considered. If you have been doing agile and something feels broken — ceremonies that waste time, estimates that lie, retrospectives that produce no change — Experience Master might be what you have been looking for.

This course teaches Experience Master from the ground up. It also shows you how Are-Self encodes Experience Master in software through its `agile_addon.py` — because Are-Self does not just use Experience Master to manage development. It runs Experience Master ceremonies as automated processes. The agile operating system is, literally, part of the AI's operating system.

## Who This Is For

Three audiences:

**PMs learning the role.** You are a Project Manager (not a scrum master — we do not use that term) who wants to understand Experience Master's philosophy, ceremonies, and tools. You may be transitioning from Scrum or starting fresh.

**Workers learning the system.** You are a Worker (not a developer — we do not use that term) who wants to understand the operating system your team runs on. You want to know what is expected of you, what you can expect from your PM, and why the ceremonies exist.

**Developers studying Are-Self.** You want to understand `agile_addon.py` — how Experience Master is encoded in software, how shifts work, and how the addon registry integrates with the rest of the system.

## Vocabulary

Experience Master uses specific terminology. This is not arbitrary — each word was chosen to carry meaning that the alternatives do not:

| Experience Master Term | What Others Call It | Why the Difference |
|----------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Iteration** | Sprint | "Sprint" implies speed. Iterations are about rhythm, not speed. You do not sprint for two weeks. |
| **Worker** | Developer | Not everyone on the team writes code. "Worker" includes QA, design, documentation, and anyone who completes work. |
| **PM** | Scrum Master | The PM has authority and accountability. The PM is a shield, not a facilitator. |
| **Sifting** | Grooming / Refinement | "Grooming" has uncomfortable connotations. "Refinement" is vague. Sifting is what you actually do: separate wheat from chaff. |
| **Iteration** | Sprint | Worth repeating. Sprints end in exhaustion. Iterations end in learning. |

Use these terms. They matter.

## Prerequisites

- **None for PMs and Workers learning the system.** No software development experience is required for Modules 1-5 and 7.
- **For understanding the Agile Addon (Module 6):** [Python Intermediate](../python-intermediate/) recommended, as the module walks through Django model code and addon registry patterns.
- Experience with any agile methodology (Scrum, Kanban, XP) is helpful but not required.

## What You Will Be Able to Do

By the end of this course, you will:

1. **Run** an Iteration from start to finish using Experience Master ceremonies
2. **Write** a Definition of Ready with all six fields, and explain why each field exists
3. **Evaluate** whether work meets the Definition of Done using the three conditions
4. **Facilitate** (as a PM) or participate in (as a Worker) every Experience Master ceremony
5. **Explain** the difference between the PM and Worker roles and why the boundary matters
6. **Read** Are-Self's `agile_addon.py` and understand how it maps to Experience Master concepts
7. **Adopt** Experience Master incrementally, starting with the retrospective

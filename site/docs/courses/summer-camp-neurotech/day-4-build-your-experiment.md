# Day 4: Build Your Own AI Experiment

**Objective:** Campers work in pairs to design and run a test exploring something the brain does that AI can't (or vice versa). This is the most camper-driven day of the week.

**Materials:**
- Paper and pens for each pair
- Computer running Are-Self with projector (pairs take turns using it)
- Whiteboard or large paper for recording experiment ideas
- Timer (phone works fine)
- Any props campers request (within reason — keep it simple)

---

## Setup (5 minutes)

Write on the board: "Today you become scientists. Your mission: find something the brain can do that AI can't — or something AI can do that the brain can't. Design a test. Run it. Prove it."

Pair up campers. If there's an odd number, one group of three is fine.

---

## Activity 1: Design the Experiment (20 minutes)

### Brainstorm (5 minutes)

"What could you test? Here are some ideas to get you started — but your own idea is probably better."

Read the list. Let pairs pick or invent their own.

**Starter ideas:**
- **Creativity test:** Ask a human and Are-Self to draw/describe something creative. Who's more original?
- **Joke test:** Tell jokes to Are-Self. Does it understand humor? Can it tell a joke that's actually funny?
- **Empathy test:** Describe a sad situation. Compare the human response to the AI response. Who connects more?
- **Speed test:** Who can answer 10 trivia questions faster — a human or Are-Self?
- **Multitask test:** Can a human hum a song while solving a math problem? Can Are-Self do two things at once?
- **Common sense test:** "If I put a ball in a cup and flip the cup over, where is the ball?" Test Are-Self's common sense vs. a human's.
- **Sarcasm test:** Can Are-Self detect sarcasm? How about a 13-year-old?
- **Deception test:** Can you lie to Are-Self? Can it tell? Can you tell when it's wrong?

### Experiment Design (15 minutes)

Each pair fills out an experiment plan on paper:

**Our question:** What are we testing?

**Our prediction:** What do we think will happen?

**The test:** What will we do, step by step? (Must include at least one thing a human does and one thing Are-Self does.)

**How we'll measure it:** How do we decide who "wins" or what the answer is?

**What we need:** Any materials or props?

Circulate and help pairs sharpen their experiments. Common issues:
- Too vague ("We'll see if the AI is smart") — push for specifics ("We'll ask it 5 riddles and score how many it gets")
- Too complicated ("We'll test 20 things") — simplify ("Pick the one test you're most excited about")
- Can't be done in 10 minutes — help them scope down

---

## Activity 2: Run the Experiment (20 minutes)

### Execution (15 minutes)

Pairs take turns at the computer to interact with Are-Self. Each pair gets 3-4 minutes of computer time. While they wait, they can:
- Run the human side of their experiment with each other
- Refine their questions
- Watch other pairs' experiments and take notes

**Instructor role during experiments:**
- Keep the line moving. Strict time per pair.
- Help with Are-Self interaction if needed (typing, navigating).
- Ask each pair: "What are you seeing? Is it what you expected?"
- Celebrate surprises — unexpected results are the best results.

### Record Results (5 minutes)

Each pair writes up their results on paper:

**What happened:** Describe the results in 2-3 sentences.

**Who won (if applicable):** Human or AI?

**What surprised us:** One thing we didn't expect.

**Our conclusion:** In one sentence, what did we learn?

---

## Activity 3: Prepare for Tomorrow (15 minutes)

### Presentation Prep (10 minutes)

"Tomorrow you present your experiment to the group. Two minutes, max. Here's what to include:"

1. **The question** (10 seconds): What did you test?
2. **The prediction** (10 seconds): What did you think would happen?
3. **What you did** (30 seconds): Describe the test.
4. **What happened** (30 seconds): Describe the results.
5. **What it means** (40 seconds): What did you learn about brains vs. AI?

Pairs practice with each other. Encourage them to be natural — this is camp, not a science fair. Personality is welcome.

### Preview: The Big Question (5 minutes)

"Tomorrow we wrap up. After presentations, we're going to tackle the biggest questions of the week: What can brains do that AI can't? What can AI do that brains can't? And does any of this matter?"

"Start thinking about your answers tonight. Your experiment today gave you evidence. Tomorrow, you get to use it."

---

## Take-Home Thought

*"A scientist doesn't start with the answer. A scientist starts with a question and designs a way to find out. You did that today. Whatever the result, you did science."*

# Day 1: What's Inside Your Head?

**Objective:** Campers learn the major brain regions, discover that someone built software organized like a brain, and start forming opinions about the brain-AI comparison.

**Materials:**
- Brain diagram (projected or printed — one per camper if possible)
- Large paper and markers (one sheet per camper)
- Bingo cards (3x3, with brain region names in the squares — pre-printed)
- Small prizes for bingo winners (stickers, candy, bragging rights)
- Computer running Are-Self with projector

---

## Setup (5 minutes)

Arrange chairs in a circle or semicircle. Post a large brain diagram where everyone can see it. Have bingo cards and markers ready but not distributed yet. Open Are-Self on the projected computer.

---

## Activity 1: Brain Anatomy Speed Round (20 minutes)

### The Hook (3 minutes)

"Your brain weighs about three pounds. It's the consistency of cold butter. It uses 20% of your body's energy even though it's only 2% of your body weight. And it's doing a hundred things right now that you have no idea about. Let's meet it."

### The Speed Tour (12 minutes)

Walk through the brain diagram. For each region, give the name, point to it, and deliver a one-liner. Keep it fast and fun — this is a speed round, not a lecture.

1. **Hippocampus** — "Memory HQ. This is where your brain decides what's worth remembering. Named after a seahorse because it looks like one."
2. **Frontal Lobe** — "The thinker. Behind your forehead. It's where you reason, decide, and plan. Also not done growing until you're 25, which explains a lot."
3. **Prefrontal Cortex** — "The boss of the frontal lobe. Impulse control, long-term planning, and deciding not to say the thing you're thinking."
4. **Hypothalamus** — "Tiny but critical. The size of an almond. Keeps your body temperature right, tells you when you're hungry, controls your hormones."
5. **Temporal Lobe** — "Sits near your temples. Language and sound processing. This is how you understand what I'm saying right now."
6. **Central Nervous System** — "Your brain and spinal cord together. The command center and the main highway."
7. **Peripheral Nervous System** — "Every nerve that isn't the brain or spinal cord. Goes all the way to your fingertips and toes."
8. **Thalamus** — "The mailroom. Every piece of sensory information (except smell) goes through the thalamus before it reaches the right brain region."
9. **Synaptic Cleft** — "The world's tiniest gap. Between every two neurons, there's a space where chemical signals jump across. Your whole brain runs on these jumps."

After each one, ask one quick question: "Who knew that? Who didn't? What surprised you?"

### The Are-Self Reveal (5 minutes)

"Here's the thing. Someone took this brain map and built software that looks like it."

Show the Are-Self brain-software map. Don't explain it in detail — just let campers see the parallel:
- "Hippocampus in your head, hippocampus in the software."
- "Frontal lobe in your head, frontal lobe in the software."
- "Hypothalamus in your head — keeps your body alive. Hypothalamus in the software — keeps the AI from spending too much money."

Let them react. "We're going to spend this week figuring out whether this comparison is brilliant or ridiculous. Maybe both."

---

## Activity 2: Draw Your Brain (20 minutes)

### Instructions (2 minutes)

Each camper gets a large sheet of paper and markers. "Draw your brain. Label everything you can remember from the speed round. If you can't remember a name, draw what it does. There is no wrong way to do this."

### Drawing Time (13 minutes)

Campers draw. Circulate and help, but mostly let them work. Encourage creativity — if someone draws the hypothalamus as a thermostat, that's great. If someone draws the synaptic cleft as a canyon with tiny people jumping across, even better.

The goal is not anatomical accuracy. The goal is engagement and recall.

### Show and Compare (5 minutes)

Everyone holds up their drawing. Quick gallery: "Who included the hippocampus? Point to it. Who drew the thalamus? Who forgot the thalamus? That's okay — I forget about the thalamus too."

Hang the drawings on the wall. They'll stay up all week as reference.

---

## Activity 3: Brain Region Bingo (15 minutes)

### Setup (2 minutes)

Distribute bingo cards. Each card is a 3x3 grid with brain region names in the squares, arranged differently on each card.

### Play (10 minutes)

Read clues — not the name of the region, but a description of what it does. Campers figure out which region matches and mark it.

**Round 1 — Biology Clues:**
- "This region makes new memories." (Hippocampus)
- "This is the body's thermostat." (Hypothalamus)
- "Every sensation except smell passes through this region." (Thalamus)
- "This system includes every nerve outside the brain and spinal cord." (PNS)
- "This is where you do your thinking and decision-making." (Frontal Lobe)
- "Chemical signals jump across this tiny gap." (Synaptic Cleft)
- "This region processes language and sound." (Temporal Lobe)
- "Your brain and spinal cord together." (CNS)
- "The part that stops you from saying something you'll regret." (Prefrontal Cortex)

**Round 2 — Software Clues:**
- "This app stores the AI's memories." (Hippocampus)
- "This app manages how much money the AI spends." (Hypothalamus)
- "This app routes messages between humans and the AI." (Thalamus)
- "This app manages the fleet of worker processes." (PNS)
- "This app does the AI's reasoning." (Frontal Lobe)
- "This app sends real-time messages between parts of the system." (Synaptic Cleft)

Prizes for bingo winners. Applause for everyone.

### Debrief (3 minutes)

"You just matched brain regions to software apps. Some of them felt obvious. Some felt weird. The weird ones are the interesting ones. We'll come back to those."

---

## Take-Home Thought

*"Your brain is doing things right now that no computer on Earth can do. But there are things computers can do that your brain can't. This week, we're going to figure out which is which."*

# Rubrics: The Human Brain and the Are-Self

## How to Use These Rubrics

Share the relevant rubric with learners at the start of each week. Use them formatively — comment on specific criteria as learners work, not just at the end. These are coaching aids, not grade sheets.

**Ladder (0→5 is growth):** 0 Blank · 1 Term Recognition · 2 Limited Awareness · 3 Applicable Awareness · 4 Synergetic · 5 Instructor Level

### Coaching Philosophy

**The goal is growth.** Track growth and celebrate progress in thinking, reasoning, and collaboration. A learner who climbs from **1 Term Recognition** to **3 Applicable Awareness** has learned more than one who sat at **4 Synergetic** without moving. These rubrics measure how learners think, not whether they're "right" — because the central question of this course doesn't have a right answer.

## AI Collaboration (Cross-Cutting)

Use this rubric throughout the course to assess how learners work with Are-Self as a research subject.

| Criteria | 0 Blank | 1 Term Recognition | 2 Limited Awareness | 3 Applicable Awareness | 4 Synergetic | 5 Instructor Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Asks thoughtful questions** | Questions missing, unclear, or closed-ended | Recognizes what a good question looks like | Present but vague or fact-based | Clear and prompt meaningful responses | Specific, open-ended, build on previous responses; ask "why" and "how" | Could teach it; models question quality for others |
| **Evaluates AI responses critically** | Accepts AI responses without question | Recognizes AI can be wrong | Inconsistent; accepts some without consideration | Evaluates most before using | Consistently checks AI output against personal knowledge | Could teach it; names specific failure modes |
| **Iterates and refines** | Treats first response as final | Recognizes iteration as an option | Iterates occasionally or with prompting | Engages in feedback loops; revises based on suggestions | Uses feedback to revise multiple times; builds on each interaction | Could teach it; models the iteration loop |
| **Documents AI's contribution honestly** | Does not document or misrepresents | Recognizes the need to document | Mentions AI use but vague about specifics | Notes where AI was used; acknowledges role | Clearly identifies what AI helped with vs. learner's own work | Could teach it; sets documentation norms |
| **Uses AI as a partner, not a replacement** | Treats AI as a replacement for thinking | Recognizes the partner/replacement distinction | Sometimes over-relies, sometimes under-uses | Uses AI to support thinking; does own work | AI extends thinking; learner does the core work; AI amplifies | Could teach it; coaches the balance |

## Tips for Using These Rubrics

1. **Share them with learners at the start of each week.** They should know what they're working toward.
2. **Use formatively.** Comment on specific criteria as learners work — don't wait until the end.
3. **Conference together.** Ask: "What does **3 Applicable Awareness** look like in your work? What would it take to reach **4 Synergetic**?"
4. **Track growth, not current level.** Moving from **1 Term Recognition** to **2 Limited Awareness** is real progress — celebrate it.
5. **The capstone rubric captures the end-of-course demo.** Weeks 1–5 are mid-course coaching checkpoints.

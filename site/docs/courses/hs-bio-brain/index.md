# The Human Brain and the Are-Self

**A six-week high school biology course that teaches neuroanatomy through a working AI mirror. Students don't just learn about the brain — they operate software built to mimic it, then figure out where the metaphor holds and where it breaks.**

## Who This Is For

You're a high school biology teacher with students who've had at least one semester of introductory bio. They know what cells are. They know organs have systems. Now you're going to show them the most complex organ in the known universe — and hand them a piece of software that was deliberately built to look like it.

Are-Self maps nine brain regions to nine Django apps. The Hippocampus stores memories. The Frontal Lobe reasons. The Hypothalamus manages resources. Students will operate the software, then turn around and study the real organ, and the gaps between the two will teach them more than either alone.

You do not need to be a computer scientist. You need to be willing to say "I don't know yet" alongside your students. That's the second habit.

## The Twelve Variables

This is a course for high schoolers, not 4th graders. They get the full scipraxian decision lattice, not the kid-scale compression:

Inclusion, Humility, Inquiry, Fulfillment or Happiness, Religion or Profit, Fun, Fear, Responsibility, Perseverance, Perception, Time, Permadeath.

Every meaningful decision weighs against all twelve. The course surfaces them naturally — when students debate whether AI "thinks," when they decide what experiment to run, when they present findings they're not sure about. The Variables aren't a unit. They're the air in the room.

## What Students Will Be Able to Do

By the end of this course, students will:

1. **Identify** the major regions of the human brain and describe their primary functions
2. **Map** each biological region to its Are-Self software analog
3. **Design** a controlled experiment testing a brain region's behavior
4. **Evaluate** where the biological metaphor holds and where it breaks down
5. **Defend** a hypothesis about what real neuroanatomy does that Are-Self doesn't
6. **Apply** the twelve scipraxian Variables to a real decision involving AI and neuroscience

## Standards Alignment (NGSS)

- **LS1.A:** Structure and Function — students analyze how brain structure relates to function at both biological and computational levels
- **LS1.D:** Information Processing — students investigate how organisms and AI systems receive and respond to information
- **SEP-1:** Asking Questions — students generate testable questions about brain-AI parallels
- **SEP-3:** Planning and Carrying Out Investigations — students design experiments to test Are-Self brain region behavior
- **SEP-4:** Analyzing and Interpreting Data — students collect and analyze data from Are-Self reasoning sessions
- **SEP-6:** Constructing Explanations — students explain where biological metaphors hold and fail
- **SEP-7:** Engaging in Argument from Evidence — students defend hypotheses about neuroanatomy gaps
- **CCC-6:** Structure and Function — the organizing theme of the entire course

## The Brain Region Map

This is the core reference students will return to every week:

| Human Brain Region | Are-Self Django App | What the Biology Does | What the Software Does |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hippocampus | `hippocampus` | Forms new memories, consolidates short-term to long-term | Stores engrams as vectors, deduplicates, tracks provenance |
| Frontal Lobe | `frontal_lobe` | Reasoning, decision-making, voluntary movement | Reasoning sessions, tool use, focus budgeting |
| Prefrontal Cortex | `prefrontal_cortex` | Planning, executive function, impulse control | Work hierarchy (epics/stories/tasks), Definition of Ready |
| Hypothalamus | `hypothalamus` | Homeostasis — temperature, hunger, hormone regulation | Model selection, cost management, circuit breakers |
| Temporal Lobe | `temporal_lobe` | Language comprehension, auditory processing, memory retrieval | Iteration scheduling, shift management, sequencing |
| Central Nervous System | `central_nervous_system` | Brain + spinal cord — command and execution | Neural pathways, neurons, spike trains |
| Peripheral Nervous System | `peripheral_nervous_system` | Nerves outside the CNS — sensation and motor control | Worker fleet management, heartbeat monitoring |
| Thalamus | `thalamus` | Sensory relay station — routes input to correct region | Chat interface, human-AI relay |
| Synaptic Cleft | `synaptic_cleft` | Gap between neurons — neurotransmitter signaling | Real-time WebSocket events, neurotransmitter signals |

## Hardware & Time Requirements

- **Minimum hardware:** One computer running Are-Self, projected for the class. Students observe and take notes collaboratively.
- **Ideal hardware:** A computer lab or 1:1 devices connected to an Are-Self server on the local network.
- **Time per day:** 50 minutes (standard high school period)
- **Total time:** 30 instructional days across 6 weeks

# Worksheets & Handouts

## Teacher Notes

These worksheets are designed to support student learning throughout the 6-week Are-Self curriculum. Each worksheet can be printed and copied for your class. Most worksheets work best when completed individually, though some (like the Feedback Comparison sheet) work well with peer discussion. All materials assume students have had at least an introductory experience with Are-Self and the concept of brain regions as functional areas. Answer keys are provided where applicable. Feel free to adapt these worksheets to match your classroom's needs and your students' developmental level.

---

## WEEK 1: Brain Regions and AI Architecture

### Worksheet 1: Brain Region Matching

**Student Name:** _________________________ **Date:** __________

Match each brain region name to its correct description. Draw a line between the region and what it does.

| Brain Region | Description |
|---|---|
| **Identity** | The tiny space between two brain cells where messages jump from one to another |
| **Synaptic Cleft** | The very tip of your brain that helps you plan, make decisions, and think about the future |
| **Temporal Lobe** | The part of your brain that stores your memories and helps you remember things |
| **Prefrontal Cortex** | The part that keeps your body running — your heartbeat, breathing, and temperature |
| **Hypothalamus** | The part that hears and understands sounds and words |
| **Central Nervous System** | Your brain and spinal cord together — the command center of your whole body |
| **Frontal Lobe** | The part that helps you think, talk, and make plans |
| **Hippocampus** | What makes YOU you — your name, your memories, your personality, and what you believe |
| **Parietal Lobe** | A messenger in your brain that sorts information and sends it to the right places |
| **Thalamus** | The part that feels touch, temperature, and where your body is in space |

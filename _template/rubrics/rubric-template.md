### Grading Philosophy

**The goal is not perfection — it's growth.** A learner who starts at
"Beginning" and ends at "Approaching" has learned more than a learner
who stays at "Exceeds." Track growth and celebrate progress in thinking,
reasoning, and collaboration.

## AI Collaboration (Cross-Cutting)

Use this rubric throughout the course to assess how learners work with
AI as a partner.

| Criteria | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaching | 1 — Beginning |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Asks thoughtful questions** | Questions are specific, open-ended, and build on previous responses. Asks "why" and "how." | Questions are clear and prompt meaningful AI responses. | Questions are present but vague or fact-based. | Questions are missing, unclear, or closed-ended. |
| **Evaluates AI responses critically** | Consistently checks AI output against personal knowledge. Does not accept suggestions blindly. | Evaluates most AI responses before using them. | Evaluates inconsistently. May accept some suggestions without consideration. | Accepts AI responses without question. |
| **Iterates and refines** | Uses AI feedback to revise work multiple times. Builds on each interaction. | Engages in feedback loops. Revises based on suggestions. | Iterates occasionally or with prompting. | Does not iterate. Treats first response as final. |
| **Documents AI's contribution honestly** | Clearly identifies what AI helped with vs. what the learner did independently. | Notes where AI was used. Acknowledges its role appropriately. | Mentions AI use but is vague about specifics. | Does not document AI use or misrepresents it. |
| **Uses AI as a partner, not a replacement** | AI extends thinking. Learner does the core intellectual work; AI amplifies it. | Uses AI to support thinking and explore ideas. Does own work. | Sometimes over-relies on AI, sometimes under-uses it. | Treats AI as a replacement for thinking. |

## Scipraxian Habits (Cross-Cutting)

| Criteria | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaching | 1 — Beginning |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Inclusion** | Actively invites others in. Seeks out and values different perspectives. Creates space for quieter voices. | Regularly asks for input. Listens to different ideas. Includes others. | Shows some inclusive behaviors but is inconsistent. | Rarely invites input from others. Works in isolation. |
| **Humility** | Confidently says "I don't know yet." Open to being wrong. Asks for help without hesitation. | Acknowledges gaps in knowledge. Open to feedback. Willing to revise. | Inconsistent. May be defensive about mistakes. | Resists acknowledging limitations. Defensive. |
| **Inquiry** | Asks one more question consistently. Pushes beyond the obvious. Persistent curiosity. | Regularly asks follow-up questions. Curious about "why" and "how." | Asks some follow-up questions but settles quickly. | Rarely asks follow-up questions. Limited curiosity. |

## Tips for Using This Rubric

1. **Share it with learners at the start of the unit.** They should know
   what they're working toward.
2. **Use formatively.** Comment on specific criteria as learners work.
3. **Conference together.** Ask: "What does a 3 look like in your work?
   What would it take to reach a 4?"
4. **Track growth, not just grades.** Moving from 1 to 2 is real progress.
5. **Make it about thinking, not perfection.** These rubrics measure how
   learners think, collaborate, and learn — not whether they're "right."
